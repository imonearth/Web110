Venus De Milo

160 to 110 BCE

Venus De Milo was discovered off of the coast of Milos, Greece in 1820. Upon discovery, the thought to be a sculpture of Aphrodite (Venus) Greek God of Love and Beauty from 160 to 110 BCE. Venus De Milo is now on display at the Louvre, in Paris France.

The Louvre


The Scream

1893 Edvard Munch

The Scream by Edvard Munch is an autobiographical work of the Symbolist Movement, as the painting depicts, it is an abstraction of Munch himself, on a walk - experiencing the chaos around him as internally he screams. This piece of art is classical, for the mere imagination and creativity of Munch. The Scream is now on display at the National Museum in Oslo.

The National Museum Oslo


The Son of Man

1946 Rene Magritte

The Son of Man was painted during the Serrealist Movement in art. Magritte painted this piece, intitally as a self portrait. By adding the apple over the top of his face, this at first realistic painting, becomes abstract and surreal. Another artist from this period includes, Salvador Dali, whom with Magritte created some of the most renowned Surrealist Paintings ever. The Son of Man is owned by a Private Collection, but sometimes is displayed at the MOMA.

The Museum of Modern Art (MOMA)
